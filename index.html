Education

The intention was to have this be the tracking system for all future work terms at NSCC, as at the time of creation, they were not tracked outside of a per-class basis.

Work

2017 - DNS Specialist (CSC)

Having finished my Systems and Network Management program, having a chance to apply that knowledge in a real world scenario was very refreshing. Serving as a DNS Specialist (title later changed to Technical Product Support Coordinator), I got to learn a lot about DNS, various security products like digital certificates and multi-factor authentication (MFA), and how integral Microsoft Excel can be to any organization.

DNS

Working through proprietary scripts and systems, I managed corporate zone files for large corporations with the utmost accuracy. With how important DNS is, there was no room for error, regardless of if I was changing one record, or 5000. Servicing clients doesn't have a curfew, so participating in a 24/7 on-call rotation ensured that all clients got the service they needed, when they needed it.

  • DNS
  • Excel

Security Products

Working hand-in-hand with DNS was several security products such as digital certificates and MFA.

CSC had partnerships with some industry leading certificate authorities, prompting a lot of relationship building between organization contacts to ensure clients got their certificates in a timely manner. Adding the requisite zone records for validation, and keeping emaculate client portfolios was key to mitigating any slow down in service, as some certificate types like extended validation can take weeks to complete.

Working with MFA involved regularly scheduled cleanups of accounts to prevent stale users from remaining in the system for long periods of time. Although this process was manual in some capacity, there were other aspects that we had semi-automated to speed up processing of orders.

  • DNS
  • Digital Certificates
  • MFA
  • Excel

Excel

Although Excel has been mentioned a few times throughout this section of my work experience, it was truly the bricks and mortar to all of the essential aspects of being a DNS Specialist. Dealing with big data on a regular basis meant that there was no alternative to being fluent in Excel and it's mandatory functions like pivot tables and vlookups. There were several automation tools we built in Excel, from zone record checkers, to traffic reporting, and even script builders.
